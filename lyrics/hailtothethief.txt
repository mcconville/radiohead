Backdrifts

1."Creep" - Pablo Honey (1993)

Radiohead's first album, Pablo Honey, is a product of it's time. It was released when grunge music was just becoming mainstream. This album is grungy and messy, with some gems such as their hit single "Creep" and the acoustic ballad "Thinking About You" In my opinion this album is the weakest of all of their albums, but once you listen to all of their albums and become a fan,  you will appreciate this classic.

2."Just" - The Bends (1995)

This album marks Radiohead's first step away from their popular, gritty, rock sound. Despite the similarities to Pablo Honey, songs like "Just", "Planet Telex', and "Street Spirit (Fade Out)" represent the change Radiohead made to their song writing. Thom Yorke's lyrics are much darker than the previous album, and there are more layers of sounds per song than Pablo Honey.

3."Paranoid Android" - Ok Computer (1997)

The song Paranoid Android is truly representative of the album Ok Computer. The song is broken down into four parts, where each band member wrote on part too it. The album still holds on to some of the grit that both Pablo Honey, but adds elements of electronic music, such as the songs "Climbing Up the Walls" and "Subterranean Homesick Alien". This is the first conceptual album that Radiohead released expressing their anti-capitalist message ["Fitter Happier"] through the idea that technology controlling every aspect of our lives. This is also represented through the new elements of sound the band includes.

4."Everything in it's Right Place" - Kid A (2000)

The album Kid A marks a major turning point in Radiohead's sound. This album is void of the grunge rock feeling that the prior albums consisted of. It is considered an innovative electronic/alternative album and is considered by many to be the their best. Songs like, "The National Anthem" use a variety of electronic devices (such as a transistor radio) layered together in order to create one chaotic sound. It takes a couple of listens to get into the album, but once you are into it this will be a fan.

5."Packt Like Sardines in a Tin Can" - Amnesiac (2001)

Amnesiac is a weird album. It holds on to the electronic feel that Kid A had, but also incorporates some of their old sound. This can be seen in the song, "I Might Be Wrong". However, songs like "Pulk/Pull Revolving Door", and "Like Spinning Plates" shows the new sound that Radiohead was adapting.

6."Myxomatosis" - Hail to the Thief (2003)

The album Hail to the Thief is Radiohead's most political album. Each song consists a subtle messages criticizing the political backdrop of the time, such as "2+2=5" and "A Wolf at the Door". The sound of this album combines the electronic vibe of Amnesiac and Kid A, as well as the more bare-boned sound The Bends consists of. In my opinion this album is one of the most under-rated albums.

7."Reckoner" - In Rainbows (2007)

In my opinion In Rainbows is the closest thing to a masterpiece that Radiohead has put out. Every song is layered perfectly and flows from one to another like a beautiful stream of sonic mastery. Songs like, "Nude", "House of Cards", and "Jigsaws Falling into Place" show that Radiohead strayed from their previous albums in order to create their very own sound. Despite songs like "Bodysnatchers" that retain the grit from Ok Computer, this album is new and fresh and requires you to listen from start to finish in order to truly understand the In Rainbows experience.

8."The Daily Mail" - The King of Limbs (2011)

The King of Limbs marks the next step in Radiohead's career. After developing their sound into something completely their own, this album tries to push that even farther. Songs like "Bloom" and "Feral" sound familiar to songs on Kid A and Amnesiac. One of the most notable songs is "Give Up the Ghost". As Radiohead developed as artists, started to layer their tracks even more, combining multiple simple melodies to create a complex song. "Give Up the Ghost" uses layering in a different way from prior albums. The song is performed by Thom York (vocalist) only. He uses a looping track of his own voice to harmonize with himself. It leaves us Radiohead fans wondering what will be the next development in their sound? The last song on the album, "Separator" surely gives hint of what to look for next.
